## Purpose

The **ORB (Organisational Reference Body)** defines the cross-functional business architecture that supports every programme and technical domain within the IDEALE-ESG X-XXXX framework. It provides the nine functional pillars required for compliant, efficient, and accountable programme execution.

## Functional Descriptions

### 00 — General
Cross-cutting policies, onboarding materials, and shared ORB templates applicable to all functional pillars.

### 01 — PMO (Programme Management Office)
Owns the Master Programme Schedule, WBS, milestone tracking, change control, and earned-value reporting across all active programmes.

### 02 — FIN (Finance and Budget Control)
Manages CAPEX/OPEX planning, budget allocation by Q-Division and ATA chapter, ROI analysis, financial risk assessment, and quarterly financial reviews.

### 03 — HR (Human Resources and Competence)
Covers workforce planning, competency frameworks, recruitment, training & development, and performance management.

### 04 — MKTG (Marketing and Communications)
Handles brand strategy, stakeholder communications, public relations, digital presence, and programme-level outreach.

### 05 — CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility)
Defines ESG commitments, sustainability reporting, community engagement, diversity & inclusion targets, and ethical supply-chain standards.

### 06 — LEG (Legal, Compliance and Contracts)
Manages contract lifecycle (NDA, MOU, SLA, subcontracting), export-control (ITAR/EAR), IP protection, regulatory compliance, and litigation risk.

### 07 — OPS (Operations and Administrative Support)
Provides facilities management, IT/tooling procurement, travel administration, document control, and general operational continuity services.

### 08 — RISK (Risk, Audit and Assurance)
Maintains the programme risk register, conducts internal audits, supports external assurance activities, and tracks corrective/preventive actions (CAPA).

### 09 — GOV (Governance, Records and Decision Ledger)
Records board and programme-level decisions, maintains the governance framework (terms of reference, delegated authorities), and archives meeting minutes and action logs.
