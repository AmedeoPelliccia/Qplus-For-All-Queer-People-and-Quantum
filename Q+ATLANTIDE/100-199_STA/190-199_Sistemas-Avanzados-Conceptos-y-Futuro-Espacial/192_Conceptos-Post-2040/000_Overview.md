# STA 190-199 · 09.192.000 — Overview

## §1 Purpose

This document serves as the entry-point for the **Post-2040 Concepts** (`192`) subsection within the Q+ATLANTIDE Space Technology Architecture (STA), code range 190-199, section 09 — Sistemas Avanzados, Conceptos y Futuro Espacial.[^baseline] It establishes the **claim-discipline framework**, **evidence-readiness requirements**, and **foresight framing** that govern all speculative and advanced concepts admitted to this subsection.[^n001]

The claim-discipline framework is binding: *post-2040 concepts require foresight framing, evidence-readiness classification, uncertainty declaration, non-hype language, reproducibility criteria and controlled transition gates before becoming architecture candidates.* No concept is admitted to architecture-candidate status without an explicit physics basis, mission context, safety case, technology-readiness screening, legal admissibility, independent review, and lifecycle traceability.[^gov]

The **no-AAA rule** (no Autonomous Authority Assumption) is active throughout this subsection: autonomous decision authority claimed by any post-2040 system concept must be explicitly gated through human-oversight governance structures before architectural admission.[^qdiv]

The safety boundary is fixed as: *future-concept critical; no operational admission without explicit physics basis, mission context, safety case, technology-readiness screening, legal admissibility, independent review and lifecycle traceability.*

Independent review is a non-negotiable prerequisite for any transition from foresight concept to architecture candidate. Concepts that invoke extraordinary claims — including faster-than-light travel, zero-point energy extraction, or instantaneous quantum communication — are classified as inadmissible absent peer-reviewed empirical evidence, regardless of technological horizon.[^archtable]
