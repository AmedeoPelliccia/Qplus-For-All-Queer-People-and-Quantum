# WP-096-1.1.1-001 — Primary Structure Architecture and Sizing

---

## 1. Purpose

This work package defines the **primary load-bearing structure architecture and sizing basis** for the AMPEL360-PLUS Spacecraft Q10 flight element.

It covers the initial sizing logic, load-path definition, material-selection basis, preliminary margins, and evidence artefacts required to support the pressure vessel, main frame, launch/ascent loads, on-orbit loads, re-entry loads, recovery loads, and crew-safety structural requirements.

---

## 2. Scope

This work package includes:

- primary structure architecture definition;
- pressure vessel structural concept;
- main load-bearing frame concept;
- preliminary sizing methodology;
- material and process selection criteria;
- launch, ascent, orbital, docking, re-entry, landing, and abort load cases;
- strength, stiffness, fatigue, damage tolerance, and fail-safe design basis;
- structural interface assumptions with TPS, propulsion, ECLSS, cabin systems, payload bay, docking/hatches, and launch vehicle interface;
- verification and evidence strategy.

This work package does **not** own:

- detailed pressure vessel qualification testing, covered by `WP-096-1.1.1-002`;
- static and dynamic load analysis campaign, covered by `WP-096-1.1.1-003`;
- TPS material selection and qualification, covered by `WP-096-1.1.3-001`;
- docking mechanism design, covered by `WP-096-1.1.4-001`;
- launch vehicle mechanical interface ownership, covered by `WP-096-2.1-001`.

## 6. Design Basis

The primary structure shall be sized against the following top-level structural drivers:

| Driver | Description |
|---|---|
| Launch / ascent loads | Axial, lateral, acoustic, vibration, shock, and separation loads |
| On-orbit loads | Docking, thermal gradients, payload transfer, pressurization, attitude-control disturbances |
| Re-entry loads | Aerothermal gradients, deceleration, vibration, control-load introduction |
| Landing / recovery loads | Touchdown, runway, sea recovery, airfield recovery, or contingency impact cases |
| Pressure vessel loads | Internal pressure, cyclic pressurization, leak-before-burst, damage tolerance |
| Crew safety | Human-rating margins, emergency load paths, survivable cabin envelope |
| Maintenance and reuse | Inspection accessibility, refurbishability, replaceable structural elements |
| TPS integration | Attachment, thermal expansion, repair zones, access constraints |

## 12. Acceptance Criteria

This work package may be considered complete when:

1. the primary structure architecture is baselined;
2. all major load paths are identified and justified;
3. preliminary sizing and mass estimates are available;
4. material candidates and allowables basis are documented;
5. interfaces with TPS, propulsion, ECLSS, payload, docking, and launch vehicle are identified;
6. verification methods are assigned to all primary structural requirements;
7. evidence artefacts are registered in the programme traceability system;
8. open assumptions are explicitly logged for CCB review.
