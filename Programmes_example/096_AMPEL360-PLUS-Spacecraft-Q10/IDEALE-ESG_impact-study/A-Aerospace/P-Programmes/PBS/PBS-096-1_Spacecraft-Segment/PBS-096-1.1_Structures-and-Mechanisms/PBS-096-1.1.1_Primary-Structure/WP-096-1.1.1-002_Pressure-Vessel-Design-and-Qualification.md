# WP-096-1.1.1-002 — Pressure Vessel Design and Qualification

**Section:** `PBS-096-1.1.1`  
**PBS node:** Primary Structure

**Programme slot:** `096`

**Primary architecture band:** `STA 110–119 — Estructuras y Materiales Espaciales`  

---

## 1. Purpose

This work package defines the **crew pressure vessel design and qualification baseline** for the AMPEL360-PLUS Spacecraft Q10 flight element.

It covers the pressure shell architecture, wall-thickness basis, frames and reinforcements, penetrations, hatches, seals, pressure-boundary interfaces, fracture-control approach, leak-before-burst logic, proof testing, qualification testing, and evidence artefacts required for human-rated operation.

---

## 2. Scope

This work package includes:

- pressure vessel architecture definition;
- pressurized volume and pressure-boundary definition;
- wall-thickness and shell-sizing methodology;
- frame, ring, bulkhead, and reinforcement concept;
- hatch, window, duct, cable, fluid, and service penetrations;
- seal, flange, feedthrough, and pressure-interface design basis;
- fracture control and damage-tolerance approach;
- leak-before-burst and safe-life / fail-safe rationale;
- proof pressure, burst pressure, cyclic pressurization, and leak-test strategy;
- qualification evidence required for human-rated pressure vessel approval.

This work package does **not** own:

- overall primary structure architecture, covered by `WP-096-1.1.1-001`;
- global static and dynamic load analysis campaign, covered by `WP-096-1.1.1-003`;
- hatch mechanism design, covered by `WP-096-1.1.4-002`;
- ECLSS atmosphere management, covered by `WP-096-1.8.1-001`;
- TPS material selection and external thermal-protection architecture, covered by `WP-096-1.1.3-001`.

## 6. Design Basis

The pressure vessel shall be designed against the following controlled drivers:

| Driver | Description |
|---|---|
| Internal pressure | Nominal, maximum, proof, emergency, and depressurization pressure cases |
| Cyclic pressurization | Repeated flight, ground test, maintenance, and contingency pressurization cycles |
| Launch and ascent loads | Combined pressure, acceleration, acoustic, vibration, and shock environments |
| Re-entry loads | Combined pressure, thermal gradient, deceleration, and aeroelastic loads |
| Penetrations | Local reinforcement around hatches, windows, feedthroughs, ducts, cables, and service ports |
| Leak-before-burst | Controlled damage progression and detectable leakage before catastrophic rupture |
| Fracture control | Crack initiation, growth, inspection interval, and residual strength logic |
| Human rating | Crew survivability, pressure integrity, emergency response time, and certification evidence |
| Maintainability | Inspection access, NDT applicability, seal replacement, and repair logic |

|---|---|

| Windows | Transparency integration, seal compression, pressure-boundary continuity |
| ECLSS | Cabin atmosphere control, pressure regulation, leak detection |
| Avionics and data handling | Sensor wiring, feedthroughs, pressure-boundary penetrations |
| Thermal protection system | Thermal gradients, attachment loads, inspection access |
| Crew systems | Seat loads, restraints, survivable cabin envelope |
| Ground support equipment | Pressure test, purge, leak check, and servicing ports |

## 8. Verification Strategy

| Verification Method | Applicability |
|---|---|
| Analysis | Wall thickness, buckling, fatigue, fracture control, thermal stress, residual strength |
| Test | Proof pressure, burst pressure, leak testing, cyclic pressurization, structural qualification |
| Inspection | Weld / bond / fastener quality, NDT, dimensional inspection, seal condition |
| Demonstration | Maintenance access, pressure servicing, leak isolation, emergency depressurization response |
| Similarity | Heritage pressure-vessel assumptions only when documented, justified, and accepted by CCB |

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

This work package may be considered complete when:

1. the pressure vessel architecture is baselined;
2. the pressure boundary is fully defined and zoned;
3. wall-thickness, frames, bulkheads, penetrations, hatches, seals, and feedthrough assumptions are documented;
4. pressure, fatigue, fracture, thermal, and residual-strength analyses are available;
5. proof, burst, leak, and cyclic pressurization verification methods are defined;
6. human-rating pressure-system evidence requirements are mapped;
7. interfaces with ECLSS, hatches, windows, avionics, TPS, and crew systems are controlled;
8. evidence artefacts are registered in the programme traceability system;
9. open assumptions are explicitly logged for CCB review.
